### Question 4

The Neural Gas algorithm updates codevectors according to:

$$w_i^{new} = w_i^{old} + \epsilon \cdot e^{-k_i/\lambda} \cdot (x - w_i^{old})$$

where:
* $w_i^{new}$ is the new value of codevector $i$.
* $w_i^{old}$ is the current value of codevector $i$.
* $\epsilon$ is the learning rate.
* $k_i$ is the rank of codevector $i$ in terms of closeness to $x$.
* $x$ is the data point currently processed by the algorithm.

Check all statements that are correct below:

* The relative movement towards $x$ is larger for codevectors closer to $x$.
* The amount of codevector movement decreases overall if $k_i$ is increased.
* In absolute terms, the most distant codevector would move by a larger amount if it were even further away from $x$.
* In relative terms, the closest codevector would move by a larger amount if it were even closer to $x$.
