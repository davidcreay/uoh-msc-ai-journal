### Question 11

Assume you use training inputs in the interval $[-1, 1]$ to construct Gaussian Process (GP) models using the squared exponential kernel.

Check all statements about this GP model that are correct:

* A function sampled from the GP model may have a positive or a negative slope at any value of $x$.
* The predicted standard deviation at $x=2$ will be greater than that predicted at $x=1$.
* The result of using the GP model to predict the mean at a given value $x$ is a random value, i.e., repeatedly computing predictions for input $x$ will not give identical results.
* For some training inputs $x$, the predicted mean will be different from the correct output $y$, unless all points are exactly on a line.
